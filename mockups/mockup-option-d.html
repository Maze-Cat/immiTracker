§ Specialty Occupation Visa

H-1B Visa

The H-1B is a non-immigrant work visa that allows US employers to hire foreign nationals in specialty occupations — typically roles requiring at least a bachelor's degree in a related field. It is the primary pathway for international professionals in tech, finance, engineering, and research to work in the United States.

Key Facts

Duration 3 years, renewable to 6 years. Extendable indefinitely with an approved I-140 petition pending.
Annual Cap 85,000 total: 65,000 general + 20,000 US master's exemption
Lottery Registration open March 1–18 each year. Computer-generated lottery in late March.
Start Date October 1st of the applicable fiscal year
Dual Intent Yes — H-1B holders may simultaneously pursue permanent residence
Cap-Exempt Universities, nonprofits affiliated with universities, and government research organizations are cap-exempt

Eligibility

The role must qualify as a "specialty occupation" — defined as one that requires theoretical and practical application of a body of specialized knowledge, and at minimum a bachelor's degree in a specific field.

  • Bachelor's degree or higher (or equivalent experience) in a relevant field
  • A qualifying employer willing to file the petition and pay prevailing wages
  • The position must qualify as a specialty occupation under USCIS definitions
  • Approved Labor Condition Application (LCA) from the Department of Labor
  • If selected in the lottery, employer must file Form I-129 by June 30

Filing Process

  1. 01
    LCA Filing (DOL) Employer submits Labor Condition Application. Typically approved within 7 business days.
  2. 02
    Lottery Registration Employer registers electronically March 1–18. USCIS runs lottery in late March. Selection results emailed within days.
  3. 03
    Full Petition Filing Employer files Form I-129 with all supporting documents by June 30 of the registration year.
  4. 04
    USCIS Adjudication Regular processing: 3–6 months. Premium processing (Form I-907): 15 business days, additional $2,805 fee.
  5. 05
    Visa Stamping (if outside US) If applying from abroad, a consular interview at a US Embassy is required to receive the visa stamp.
  6. 06
    Begin Employment Work may begin October 1st (or later if approval arrives after that date).

Path to Green Card

Most H-1B holders pursue an employment-based green card through their employer. The typical sequence is: PERM labor certification → I-140 petition → Adjustment of Status (I-485) when a visa number is available. Processing times vary widely by country of birth due to annual per-country limits.
