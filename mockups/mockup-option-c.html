§ 03 — Specialty Occupation

H-1B Visa

The H-1B is a non-immigrant visa that allows US employers to temporarily employ foreign workers in specialty occupations. Most tech, finance, and engineering roles qualify. Annual cap of 85,000 with lottery selection each April.

Duration 3 years, extendable to 6 years (and beyond with green card pending)
Annual Cap 65,000 regular + 20,000 US master's exemption
Lottery Period Registration opens early March; lottery in late March
Start Date October 1st of the fiscal year
Dual Intent Yes — you may pursue permanent residence simultaneously

Eligibility Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree or equivalent in a relevant specialty field
  • A qualifying employer willing to file and sponsor the petition
  • The role must qualify as a specialty occupation under USCIS definitions
  • Employer must pay the prevailing wage for the position and location
  • Labor Condition Application (LCA) approved by the Dept. of Labor

Filing Process

01
LCA Filing Employer submits Labor Condition Application to DOL (typically 7 days)
02
Lottery Registration Employer registers electronically Mar 1–18. USCIS runs lottery in late March.
03
Petition Filing If selected, employer files Form I-129 with supporting documents by Jun 30.
04
USCIS Adjudication Regular processing: 3–6 months. Premium processing: 15 business days.
05
Visa Stamp (if abroad) Consular interview required if applying from outside the US.
06
Begin Employment Status begins October 1st or petition approval date, whichever is later.

§ 03

Common Questions

Your priority date is the date USCIS received your immigrant petition (Form I-140 or labor certification). It acts as your place in line for a green card. When the Visa Bulletin's cutoff date advances past your priority date, you may proceed with the final steps of your green card application.
The Department of State publishes a new Visa Bulletin around the 10th of each month, covering the following month. Dates can move forward (advancement), stay the same, or move backward (retrogression).
Final Action Dates are when USCIS can actually approve your green card. Dates for Filing (also called "Adjustment of Status Filing Dates") are an earlier threshold that, when authorized by USCIS, allow you to file Form I-485 even before a visa number is immediately available. USCIS announces each month whether to use the Dates for Filing chart.
Yes. If demand for visa numbers in a category exceeds supply, the State Department may move the cutoff date backward. This temporarily makes your priority date "not current" again, pausing your ability to file or receive approval. Retrogression is particularly common in the EB-2 and EB-3 India and China categories.
