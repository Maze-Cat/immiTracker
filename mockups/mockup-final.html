H-1B Visa

Specialty Occupation — Technology, Finance, Healthcare, Engineering & more

Annual Cap
85,000
Duration
3 → 6 yrs
FY2026 Status
Closed

FY2027 Heads Up: USCIS typically opens H-1B electronic registration in early March. Registration is expected March 2026 for FY2027 employment start (Oct 1, 2026). Set a reminder so you don't miss the window.
Eligibility Requirements
✓
Hold a U.S. bachelor's degree (or equivalent) in a directly related specialty field
✓
Have a US employer willing to sponsor you and file a petition on your behalf
✓
The position must qualify as a "specialty occupation" requiring specialized knowledge
✓
Employer must pay the prevailing wage determined by the Department of Labor
✓
Labor Condition Application (LCA) must be certified by DOL before petition filing
Application Timeline
1
Employer files Labor Condition Application (LCA)
~7 days
Submitted to DOL's FLAG system. Employer attests to paying the prevailing wage for the role and location.
2
Electronic Registration Window
Mar 1–20
Employer submits a registration with basic information about you during USCIS's registration period.
3
USCIS Lottery (if oversubscribed)
Late March
Random selection from all eligible registrations. ~65,000 regular cap + 20,000 U.S. master's exemption.
4
File Form I-129 Petition
Apr 1 – Jul 1
If selected, employer files a full petition with supporting documents and filing fees.
5
Receive USCIS Decision
2–6 months
Approval notice (I-797A) received. Premium processing ($2,805) guarantees 15-business-day decision.

H-1B FAQs

Plain-language answers to the most frequently asked questions.

What happens if I'm not selected in the H-1B lottery? +
If you're not selected, you have options. Your employer can try again next year. You might also look into cap-exempt employers (universities, nonprofits), the O-1A visa if you have extraordinary achievements, or the L-1 if you're with a multinational company. Many people get approved in subsequent years.
