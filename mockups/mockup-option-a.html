H-1B Visa
Specialty Occupation — Temporary Work Authorization
FY2026 Closed
Next filing: Apr 2026
⚠ FY2027 Registration Period: USCIS has not yet announced the FY2027 H-1B registration dates. Monitor this page or subscribe to alerts for updates.
Eligibility Requirements
  • ✓Hold a U.S. bachelor's degree (or equivalent) in a directly related field of specialty
  • ✓Employer must be a US-based entity willing to sponsor and file a petition on your behalf
  • ✓Position must qualify as a "specialty occupation" requiring theoretical or technical expertise
  • ✓Employer must pay the prevailing wage as determined by DOL for the position and location
  • ✓Labor Condition Application (LCA) must be certified by the Department of Labor
Application Process
  • 1
    Employer files Labor Condition Application (LCA)
    Employer submits LCA to DOL's FLAG system requesting prevailing wage determination
    ~7 days
  • 2
    Register for H-1B Electronic Registration
    Employer submits electronic registration during the designated window (typically March)
    Mar 1–20
  • 3
    USCIS Lottery Selection
    USCIS conducts computer-generated lottery if registrations exceed the 65,000 + 20,000 cap
    Late March
  • 4
    File Form I-129 Petition
    If selected, employer files full I-129 petition with supporting documentation
    Apr 1–Jul 1
  • 5
    Receive USCIS Decision
    Approval Notice (I-797A) issued. Premium Processing available for 15-business-day decision.
    2–6 months

Frequently Asked Questions — H-1B

What happens if I am not selected in the H-1B lottery?
If you are not selected, your employer may re-register you in future fiscal year lotteries. You may also consider alternative visa options such as O-1A (Extraordinary Ability), L-1 (if eligible for intracompany transfer), or cap-exempt employer sponsorship through universities or nonprofits affiliated with research.
Can I change employers while on H-1B?
Yes. Under the H-1B portability rule (AC21), you may begin working for a new employer as soon as they file an H-1B transfer petition on your behalf, provided your current H-1B has been approved and valid for at least 180 days.
Does H-1B lead to a green card?
The H-1B does not automatically lead to a green card, but it is a common pathway. Many H-1B holders pursue employer-sponsored green cards through the EB-2 or EB-3 employment-based preference categories simultaneously with H-1B status.
